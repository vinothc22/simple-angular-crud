The Decumulation tool splits the total investment amount between two portfolios, one to protect against markets falls in the initial years and the other to provide long term growth. The split is dependent on the gross income rate required and the consequential need to balance sequencing and shortfall risk. Sequencing risk is when a market fall early on in your retirement leads you to withdrawing a larger percentage than initially envisaged. Shortfall risk is when your portfolio does not grow fast enough to sustain your retirement and you end up exhausting your savings prematurely. The more you protect against one of these risks the greater the increase in the other risk, the illustrator finds the best compromise between the two.

Stochastic modelling is used to run through 1,000 different theoretical market scenarios to assess the feasibility of the desired income rate. The results of this are illustrated in the income viability table below, which illustrate the number of scenarios that have been exhausted in any one year. For example, an income viability of 30% in year 40, means that 700 of the 1,000 scenarios have run out of money by this time. This suggests that in 70% of scenarios you will have to reduce your income requirements to prevent this happening. For this reason, regular reviews are recommended.

It is important that investors are comfortable with the risks that they are taking and the chances that they may need to modify their desired income rate.
